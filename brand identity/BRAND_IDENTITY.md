# Holy Pav Brand Identity

## Brand Core
- Brand essence: A loud, street-smart, Mumbai-style pav brand with a playful spicy edge.
- Personality: Bold, unapologetic, energetic, handcrafted, and slightly rebellious.
- Visual intent: High-contrast red typography on warm light surfaces with sharp spicy-green accents.

## Logo Language (Derived From Provided Logo)
- Dominant wordmark treatment: Heavy condensed uppercase text (`HOLY PAV`) in strong red.
- Supporting motifs: Halo/burger icon and chilli/splash cues representing indulgence + spice.
- Texture direction: Slightly gritty/stamped vibe over clean flat minimalism.

## Color System
Primary palette:
- Brand Red: `#B20D0D`
- Deep Red: `#860000`
- Warm Paper: `#FFF8F1`
- Warm Surface Tint: `#FFF1E8`
- Dark Ink: `#2B1912`

Secondary accent palette:
- Bun Gold: `#F2A52F`
- Chilli Green: `#4F8D2F`
- Soft Line: `#E5CEB7`

Usage rules:
- Use red for all primary calls-to-action and brand headlines.
- Use warm paper tones for large surfaces and cards.
- Use green sparingly for “fresh/spicy/success” moments.
- Keep neutral text warm (brown/ink), not cool gray.

## Typography
- Display font stack: `Anton`, `Bebas Neue`, `Haettenschweiler`, `Impact`, `Arial Narrow Bold`, `sans-serif`
- Body font stack: `Sora`, `Avenir Next`, `Trebuchet MS`, `Noto Sans`, `sans-serif`

Type rules:
- Headlines: Condensed uppercase display style with tight leading.
- Navigation/buttons: Uppercase with tracking for punchy utility text.
- Body copy: Legible, modern sans-serif with warm neutral text color.

## UI Direction Implemented
- Sticky branded header with bold `HOLY PAV` mark style.
- Layered warm background with red/gold/green atmospheric gradients.
- Pill-based CTA system with red primary actions.
- Card surfaces and borders shifted to warm-paper palette.
- Entry motion with short vertical rise + fade for section staging.

## Consistency Checklist For Future Work
- Use red-led contrast first, then warm neutrals, then green accents.
- Prefer condensed uppercase headings over generic serif/sans display styles.
- Keep rounded cards/buttons but avoid soft pastel aesthetics.
- Introduce at least one bold focal element per page (headline block, brand art, or CTA band).
- Avoid introducing cool blue/purple as primary accents.
